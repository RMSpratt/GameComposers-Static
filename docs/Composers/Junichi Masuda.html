Biography

Junichi Masuda is a Japanese composer for and the director of many games in the Pokémon game series. He is currently still employed by Game Freak, having joined the company at its inception in 1989. Despite taking a more active role in the games' music early on, Masuda has shifted to handle different roles in the games' production to let new musicians offer a fresh take on the series' music.

Masuda was interested in music from a very young age, and in elementary school would try to play songs he liked on the recorder. However, Masuda didn't study music in school, but did partake in a brass band. While working in an office setting, he was approached by Satoshi Tajiri who would recruit him for the starting company Game Freak. The first game he composed for here was Mendel Palace in 1990.

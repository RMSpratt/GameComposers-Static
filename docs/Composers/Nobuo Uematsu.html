Biography

Nobuo Uematsu is a Japanese composer best-known for his work in the Final Fantasy series. He was employed by the Game Development studio Square (now Square Enix), from 1985 - 2004 before leaving to work freelance. He currently owns a production company known as Smile Please. Despite leaving Square Enix, Uematsu still regularly provides music for them in the Final Fantasy series.

In his early years, Uematsu received an English degree from Kanagawa University, and played in several bands. While playing in one of these bands at a local pub, he was recruited by a representative of Square. His first official contribution for the company was Cruise Chaser Blassty, but his breakout wokr would be Final Fantasy released in 1987. Since then, he has been a part of nearly all of the mainline games in the series.

Outside of his work in games, Uematsu has also composed for a few movies and tv shows such as Ah! My Goddess: The Movie and Blue Dragon. He has also performed in live concerts such as the 2003 Symphonic Game Music Concert.

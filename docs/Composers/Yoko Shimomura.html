Biography

Yoko Shimomura is a Japanese composer and arranger who has worked in many widely-known video games and video game series, such as: Kingdom Hearts, Mario & Luigi and Super Smash Bros. Shimomura first started composing music for video games after being hired by game studio Capcom as a student. At the time of being hired, Shimomura was studying to become a piano instructor.

While at Capcom, Shimomura contributed to sixteen games, some for gaming consoles, and others as arcade games. In 1993, she would leave Capcom to join another game development studio, Square. Shimomura states that she left to be able to write a more classical style of music in a different genre of games. While working for Square, Shimomura wrote the soundtracks to one of the most influential games in her early career, Kingdom Hearts. However, despite her success with the company, Shimomura would leave in 2003 to work freelance following a maternity leave. Shimomura still has ties with the studio though, and regularly contributes music to the Kingdom Hearts series.

Biography

Mahito Yokota is a Japanese composer, arranger, and occasional sound director who has contributed to video game music since 1998. Yokota grew up playing the piano and received orchestration and composition training before becoming interested in creating video game music. It wasn't until after he had graduated that he would begin working for former video game publisher Koei (now rebranded as Koei Temco), before joining Nintendo in 2003.

After joining Nintendo, Yokota's first contribution was for the game Donkey Kong Jungle Beat, but his first major debut title would be Super Mario Galaxy. This game would be a major challenge for Yokota, as he was under the supervision of veteran composer Koji Kondo, who ensured that he was capturing the essence of Mario in the game's soundtrack.

Following Super Mario Galaxy, Yokota has gone on to contribute to quite a few of Nintendo's major franchises, showcasing his orchestral style.

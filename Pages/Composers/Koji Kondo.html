Biography

Koji Kondo is a veteran Japanese composer responsible for creating some of the most recognized songs in gaming history, including the original themes from Super Mario Bros., The Legend of Zelda, and many other notable Nintendo games and franchises. He has been working exclusively on Nintendo titles since 1984.

Despite not receiving any formal music training, Kondo had a background in electronic music from a young age before being recruited by Nintendo. His first official game assignment was Punch-Out, and would soon be tasked with creating songs for some of Nintendo's most notable franchises. A big influence on Kondo's musical style is ensuring that the music he writes fits not only the setting, but also the gameplay itself.

In more recent years, Kondo has taken more of a supervising role, as he often coaches new composers to make sure their new music reflects the franchise that they're scoring for. A notable documented example is with Mahito Yokota for Super Mario Galaxy.

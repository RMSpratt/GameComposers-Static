Biography

Grant Kirkhope was born July 10th, 1962 in Edinburgh, Scotland. His musical background before working on video games include playing in the rock bands "Little Angels" and "Tribe", as well as receiving classical training while attending The Royal Northern College of Music.

Kirkhope was originally employed by Rare in October 1995, marking his first job as a video game composer. The first game he officially contributed music for was Donkey Kong Land 2, where he arranged David Wise's songs from Donkey Kong Country 2: Diddy's Kong Quest to fit the Gameboy. Since then, Grant Kirkhope has worked for numerous well-known gaming companies including Rare, Nintendo, Microsoft, and Playtonic Games.

Kirkhope's musical style has varied a lot over the years. Some of his earlier works such as Banjo-Kazooie, Banjo-Tooie, and Donkey Kong 64 showcase his ability to write highly memorable and catchy tunes. His more recent works showcase his other bigger and more grandeur orchestral style as seen in Mario & Rabbids: Kingdom Battle, Yooka-Laylee, and Banjo Kazooie: Nuts & Bolts.
